# Vocabulary Learning Guideline for Pre-intermediate Learners

## 1. Main Goal

At the pre-intermediate level, the goal is not to learn many difficult words. The main goal is to learn common and useful words, understand their meanings, pronounce them correctly, and use them in simple sentences.

You should learn vocabulary step by step:

**understand the word → pronounce it → write it down → use it in a sentence → revise it many times**

---

## 2. Weekly Study Routine

Choose one vocabulary unit each week.

Spend about **30–45 minutes** studying the new unit.

When you study a new unit, do not only read the words. You need to be active. This means you should read aloud, write, check, repeat, and use the words.

### Steps for studying a new unit

1. Read the title of the unit.
2. Look at the topic and guess what kind of vocabulary you will learn.
3. Read the new words and phrases carefully.
4. Say each new word aloud.
5. Highlight important or difficult words.
6. Write the words in your vocabulary notebook.
7. Write the meaning in English or Vietnamese.
8. Write one example sentence for each important word.
9. Do the exercises.
10. Check your answers and mark difficult words for revision.

---

## 3. Daily Revision Routine

Revise vocabulary every day for **5–10 minutes**.

Revision is very important because you can forget new words quickly if you do not see them again.

### Steps for daily revision

1. Look at the English word.
2. Cover the meaning.
3. Try to remember the meaning.
4. Say the word aloud.
5. Read your example sentence.
6. Try to make a new sentence.
7. Mark the words you still forget.
8. Come back to those words after five minutes.

Do not worry if you forget some words. Forgetting is normal. It only means you need to revise those words again.

---

## 4. How to Use a Vocabulary Notebook

Your vocabulary notebook should be clear and organized.

Do not write all new words in one long list. It is better to organize words by topic.

### Example topic organization

| Topic | Words and Phrases |
|---|---|
| Food | flour, raw, frozen, diet |
| Work | permanent job, advice, communicate |
| Daily life | take a break, lay the table, spend time |

For each new word, you should write:

1. The English word
2. The part of speech
3. The meaning
4. The Vietnamese translation if necessary
5. One example sentence
6. Useful word partners
7. Synonyms or opposites if possible
8. Word family if possible

### Example vocabulary note

| Word | Part of speech | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| advice | noun | an opinion that helps someone decide what to do | My teacher gave me useful advice. |

---

## 5. Learn Words with Example Sentences

Do not learn single words only. Always try to learn words in sentences.

A sentence helps you understand:

- the meaning of the word
- the grammar of the word
- the words that often go with it
- how to use it naturally

Example:

Do not only learn:

> **revise = ôn lại**

Learn:

> **I revise English vocabulary every morning.**

This helps you remember and use the word more easily.

---

## 6. Learn Word Partners

Word partners are words that often go together. They are also called **collocations**.

Learning word partners helps your English sound more natural.

### Examples

| Word | Useful word partners |
|---|---|
| advice | give advice, ask for advice, useful advice |
| break | take a break, have a break, lunch break |
| time | spend time, waste time, save time |
| mistake | make a mistake, correct a mistake |

When you learn a new word, ask yourself:

> **What words often go with this word?**

---

## 7. Learn Word Families

A word family is a group of related words.

Learning word families helps you expand your vocabulary faster.

### Examples

| Basic word | Word family |
|---|---|
| advise | advice |
| different | difference, differ |
| communicate | communication, communicative |
| enjoy | enjoyment, enjoyable |
| revise | revision |

When you learn a new word, try to find its noun, verb, adjective, or adverb form.

---

## 8. Learn Synonyms and Opposites

A synonym is a word with a similar meaning.

An opposite is a word with a different meaning.

### Examples

| Word | Synonym | Opposite |
|---|---|---|
| clean | tidy | dirty |
| difficult | hard | easy |
| kind | nice | unkind |
| permanent | lasting | temporary |

This method helps you remember more words from one word.

---

## 9. Use a Dictionary Correctly

Use both a bilingual dictionary and an English dictionary.

A bilingual dictionary is useful because it is quick and easy to understand.

An English dictionary is useful because it gives you English definitions, example sentences, pronunciation, and common word partners.

When you use a dictionary, check:

1. Meaning
2. Pronunciation
3. Part of speech
4. Example sentences
5. Word partners
6. Different meanings of the word

Do not only read the first meaning. Many English words have more than one meaning.

---

## 10. Guess Before Checking the Dictionary

When you see a new word in a text, do not check the dictionary immediately.

First, try to guess the meaning from the sentence.

Then, use the dictionary to check if your guess is correct.

This will help you become a better reader.

---

## 11. Simple 30-Minute Daily Study Plan

If you have 30 minutes a day, use this plan:

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 5 minutes | Revise old words |
| 10 minutes | Learn new words |
| 5 minutes | Check pronunciation |
| 5 minutes | Write example sentences |
| 5 minutes | Test yourself |

This plan is short but effective if you follow it every day.

---

## 12. Simple 10-Minute Study Plan

If you are busy, use this short plan:

1. Revise 5 old words.
2. Learn 3 new words.
3. Write 1 example sentence.
4. Say the words aloud.
5. Test yourself quickly.

Even 10 minutes a day is useful if you do it regularly.

---

## 13. Weekly Review

At the end of each week, review the unit again.

Ask yourself:

1. How many words can I remember?
2. Which words are still difficult?
3. Can I use the words in my own sentences?
4. Do I know the pronunciation?
5. Do I know useful word partners?
6. Do I need to revise this unit again?

You should not move too quickly to the next unit if you cannot remember or use the important words from the current unit.

---

## 14. Final Learning Rule

For pre-intermediate learners, vocabulary learning should be simple, active, and regular.

Do not only read words.

You should:

**read → say → write → use → revise → test yourself**

If you follow this routine every week, your vocabulary will become stronger and you will be able to use English more confidently.
